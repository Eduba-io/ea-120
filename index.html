01 / The Eduba frame

Primary-source collection raises the bar on every layer above it.

We work on a simple idea. About 60% of what an analyst workflow needs is traditional code and database work. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is a genuine AI problem. The job is sorting those three into the right layers before anything ships, and documenting the sort so the analyst who puts their byline on the output can defend it.

Applied to the Flashpoint stack, the map resolves quickly. Primary-source ingestion across deep and dark web, chat platforms, and OSINT feeds is a database-and-pipeline layer. Vulnerability intelligence from the RBS heritage is a structured data problem. Tradecraft rules (what to elevate, which source chain is permissible in a customer deliverable) are rule-based logic. The genuinely agentic surface is the analyst workflow assist, and it only holds trust if the three layers below it are drawn cleanly.

That is the work we would propose to do with the product and intel team before the next GTIR cadence.
